About Me:

Born in Puerto Rico, but lived in Iowa long enough to call it home also. After leaving Iowa I spent some time in Washington, D.C. and Charleston, S.C. I then went to work for Microsoft as a Data & Applied Scientist II for their Fraud, Waste & Abuse Operations, bouncing between Puerto Rico and Redmond, W.A. In 2019 I came back to Washington, D.C. as a White House Presidential Innovation Fellow detailee to the Department of Veterans Affairs. I lead the deployment of the first ever AI API at the Department of Veterans Affairs that reduced Veteran wait time on benefit decisions by a week and saved the VA a recurrent 10,000,000$/year. I also received a Special Act Award for my work at the VA and was a FedScoop 50 Nominee for Disruptor of The Year in 2019 and for Federal Leadership in 2020. Due to one glorious error on a NASA event program for which I was a keynote, I became the first and only Presidential Space Fellow (I got a picture to prove it)!

I am currently on my third year of my tour of duty, serving as an Entrepreneur in Residence detailed primarily to the Intelligent Transportation Systems - Joint Program Office at the Department of Transportation helping draft their AI & Cybersecurity Strategies. I also served as an AI, Data, and Cybersecurity SME for NASA, NSF, DoD, GSA, and OSTP. Below you'll find some of the press written about my work.

When I'm not working on improving government you can find me playing gigs around the DC area.
